COURSE STRUCTURE

Theoretical Instruction: Engaging lectures, multimedia presentations, and interactive discussions facilitated by experienced nursing faculty. Laboratory Sessions: Hands-on practice in a simulated healthcare environment to reinforce essential nursing skills and techniques. Clinical Practicum: Real-world experience in healthcare facilities under the supervision of qualified preceptors, allowing students to apply classroom knowledge in clinical settings.
KEY LEARNING
  • Gain a thorough understanding of the nursing process, including assessment, diagnosis, planning, implementation, and evaluation.
  • Develop proficiency in fundamental nursing skills such as vital signs assessment, medication administration, wound care, and patient hygiene.
  • Learn effective communication techniques for interacting with patients, families, and interdisciplinary healthcare teams.
  • Explore concepts of patient safety, infection control, and ethical decision-making in nursing practice.
  • Understand the role of evidence-based practice in delivering high-quality nursing care and promoting positive patient outcomes.
  • Cultivate critical thinking and problem-solving abilities to address complex healthcare challenges in diverse clinical settings.
